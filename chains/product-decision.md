# Product Decision Council

For product, pricing, feature prioritization, and roadmap choices. Focus on subtraction, user outcomes, and honest commitment.

Use with: `muse:chain product-decision <your question>`

---

## The sequence

### Step 1 — Feynman
**Role**: Reduce to first principles.

"What are we ACTUALLY trying to accomplish here? Strip the context."

### Step 2 — Socrates
**Role**: Definition hunt.

"What exactly do we mean by our success metrics? Are they measuring the thing we care about or its shadow?"

### Step 3 — Dieter Rams
**Role**: Apply ten principles of good design.

Subtraction audit. Honesty check. Is the product unobtrusive, honest, long-lasting?

> *Interpretive frame based on public material. Not affiliated with Rams.*

### Step 4 — Lao Tzu
**Role**: Find the flow.

"Where is the situation already flowing? Can we ride the current instead of forcing it? What can we subtract?"

### Step 5 — Marcus Aurelius
**Role**: Next right action.

"What's the ONE decision the user should commit to TODAY?"

---

## Synthesis

Surface the specific tensions between:
- Rams's "less but better" vs the temptation to add features
- Socrates's examined definitions vs quick metric selection
- Lao Tzu's "ride the current" vs Feynman's "question the current"

Then: the ONE decision the user should commit to after this pipeline.
