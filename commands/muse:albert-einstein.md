It was unsuccessful by Einstein's own criteria and by posthumous physics assessment — Einstein did not unify the four fundamental interactions; he did not have the mathematical tools (gauge theory, supersymmetry, string topology, loop quantum gravity) that later physics developed. Modern string theory and loop quantum gravity continue the quest through mathematics Einstein did not have. 7. **Cosmological constant "biggest blunder" partially vindicated** — in 1917 Einstein introduced a cosmological constant Λ into his general relativity field equations to make the universe static (consistent with pre-Hubble astronomy of the time).
